Before you click read our Career TIPS

According to surveys, each recruiter devotes 30 seconds to each CV. How are you going to do it? Read the tips below to ensure that your CV will attract interest.

Who Am I

Start by writing your full name and contact details at the top of the page. Email, contact phone number, address and LinkedIn are all you need to report.

Photos and personal data, such as marital status, date of birth, etc. are information you do not need to report.

Moreover, if you want, you can write briefly some elements that characterize you.

E.g.: Katerina Sotirou
Email: ksotir121@gmai.com, 6978888888, K. Halandriou 12, 12125, Athens

Competencies: Creative, Self-Motivated, Detail and Results Oriented

Education

Write down the subject of your studies, which educational institution and when.

Pay attention and mention the educational institution, the school/department and the period of time you studied. Finally, if there are significant achievements - scholarships, accolades, degree, etc.

If you're still studying or doing a master's degree, let us know! It is better for the recruiter to know in advance than to find out during the interview.

Training

Briefly mention the seminars and workshops you attended.
E.g.: Digital Marketing Camp, Kariera, 2019

Systems and Additional Knowledge

Mention additional knowledge you have in programs, systems, foreign languages certifications or degrees.
E.g.: English: IELTS, French: B2, Microsoft Office: Word, Excel, Other Systems: SAP

Volunteering and Hobbies

You can mention your hobbies and your volunteer activities.
E.G.: Volunteer at Ted-X, Hobby: Volleyball

Work experience

Write in chronological order from the most recent in your earlier work.

Mention the company you worked for, the role you had, and make a brief description of your daily tasks and the projects you took or participated in. In addition, inform us regarding the length of time you worked at each company. Finally, if there are significant achievements, you can make a short mention and either underline them or bold them to emphasize.
